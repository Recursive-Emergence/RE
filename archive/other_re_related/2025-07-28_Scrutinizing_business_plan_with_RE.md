## **1. What Is Narrative Entropy?**
In RE terms【13†thesis.md】【15†chapter_6_cognitive_layer_morality.md】, a narrative reduces entropy when it:
- **Compresses complex events into coherent, reusable structures** (e.g., an investigative report distilling chaos into cause/effect chains).  
- **Resolves contradictions or integrates multiple perspectives** (reducing informational disorder).  
- **Becomes a node in a larger lattice** (reused and referenced across stories).  

Thus, **narrative entropy ($H_n$)** measures the **degree of disorder, contradiction, and fragmentation** in a story (or story cluster).  

---

## **2. Mathematical Framing**

We can define **Narrative Entropy** as a function of:
- **Contradiction density ($C_d$):** Degree of unresolved conflict among narrative elements (claims, sources).  
- **Coherence ($\kappa$):** Semantic alignment and logical flow of narrative.  
- **Source dispersion ($S_d$):** Diversity and independence of sources.  
- **Stake dispersion ($D_s$):** Breadth and diversity of community confidence signals (who’s putting value behind it).

A possible starting formula:

\[
H_n = \alpha \cdot C_d - \beta \cdot \kappa - \gamma \cdot D_s - \delta \cdot S_d
\]

Where:
- $\alpha, \beta, \gamma, \delta$ are tunable weights.  
- **Lower $H_n$** = more coherent, validated, and integrated narrative.  
- **Higher $H_n$** = chaotic, fragmented, or contested narrative.  

---

### **2.1 Contradiction Density ($C_d$)**
Quantify conflicts among story elements:
\[
C_d = \frac{\sum_{i,j} w_{i,j} \cdot \mathbb{1}(p_i \neq p_j)}{N_p^2}
\]
- $p_i$ = proposition/claim in narrative  
- $w_{i,j}$ = importance weight (based on prominence in story)  
- $N_p$ = number of propositions  

### **2.2 Coherence ($\kappa$)**
Use semantic embedding similarity between sentences/sections:
\[
\kappa = \frac{1}{N_s^2} \sum_{i,j} \mathrm{sim}(e_i, e_j)
\]
- $e_i$ = sentence embedding  
- $\mathrm{sim}()$ = cosine similarity  

### **2.3 Source Dispersion ($S_d$)**
Encourage multiple independent sources:
\[
S_d = 1 - \sum_k p_k^2
\]
- $p_k$ = proportion of narrative attributed to source $k$ (Shannon diversity index form).  

### **2.4 Stake Dispersion ($D_s$)**
Measure diversity of stakeholder groups funding/staking:
\[
D_s = 1 - \sum_g q_g^2
\]
- $q_g$ = proportion of total stake from group $g$ (e.g., local vs. diaspora vs. institutions).
