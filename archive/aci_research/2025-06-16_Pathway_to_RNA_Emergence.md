### 1. **High Entropic Baseline (Low Starting Structure)**

- Most molecular interactions are **random, short-lived, and non-recursive**.
- **Spontaneous order** is *statistically rare* unless there's a local attractor (e.g., surface, enclosure, or catalytic site).

🔁 *Recursive emergence requires local entropy reduction* — but almost everything naturally increases entropy unless constrained.

### 2. **Tiny Probabilities + Vast Combinatorics**

- The **space of random nucleotide chains** of length 50+ is astronomical (~4⁵⁰ possible chains).
- The chance that one of these chains has **catalytic self-replicating function** is **<1 in 10⁶⁰**, or worse.
- Even if ribose or adenine forms, it’s unstable or degrades easily.

⚠️ **Functional recursion is not just chemical stability — it's structure + memory + action**. Rare, composite, and self-reinforcing.

In the table:

- A **negative value (−)** means the reaction **releases energy** (exothermic)  
- A **positive value (+)** means the reaction **requires energy input** (endothermic)

## 🔥 Breakdown of Examples

### **1. CO₂ + H₂ → HCHO (formaldehyde)**
- **ΔE ≈ -20 kJ/mol** → Exothermic
- This reaction releases energy as CO₂ is reduced to a simpler organic molecule (formaldehyde).
- In RE terms: **small entropy drop**, low reusability, but it's a **favorable spontaneous step** toward complexity.

### **2. NH₃ + CH₄ + H₂O + Spark → HCN, HCHO, amino acids**
- **ΔE ≈ +400 kJ/mol** → Endothermic
- Requires a lot of energy — e.g., electrical discharge or UV light.
- This is a classic **non-spontaneous reaction** unless driven by high-energy events (like lightning).
- Despite the energy cost, it **yields molecules with lower entropy and more reusability** — key for early life.
