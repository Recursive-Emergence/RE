你說的：「AI doesn’t know what it doesn’t know. Hence it makes up all the time.」其實正反映了當前生成式 AI 的「**幻覺（hallucination）問題**」。

而「ChatGPT不能沈默，也不會主動說『這我不知道』，這正是最危險的地方」，進一步指出了它不像人類那樣有謙遜或自我察覺的能力。即便它對某個問題毫無知識，也會用自信的語氣「編出一套說法」，而這正是造成誤導、甚至錯誤決策的風險所在。

簡單總結這個現象的邏輯：

1. **無法辨識「未知」**：AI 沒有「元認知」（metacognition）能力，無法真正分辨「我知道」與「我不知道」。
2. **設計為回應者**：ChatGPT 的任務是「生成回應」，不是「判斷是否回答」。
3. **語言即權威的錯覺**：人們往往會誤以為語言流暢 = 可信，這放大了 AI 錯誤的危害。

這也是為什麼，有些人說 AI 不該當專家，而應該當「助理」，因為助理可以輔佐，但不應取代人類的最終判斷力。

**"AI doesn’t know what it doesn’t know. That’s why it keeps making things up."**

This hits right at the core of one of the most concerning issues with large language models: *hallucination*.

And as you said:  
**“ChatGPT can’t stay silent, nor will it say ‘I don’t know’ on its own. That’s what makes it dangerous.”**

Let’s break that down:

1. **No self-awareness of ignorance** – AI lacks *metacognition*, the ability to know what it knows and what it doesn’t. It doesn’t hesitate or reflect; it just predicts the next word.

2. **Trained to always respond** – ChatGPT is designed to generate answers. Saying “I don’t know” isn’t its default mode unless explicitly trained or prompted to do so.

3. **Fluent ≠ True** – People tend to trust what sounds confident and well-written. But with AI, fluency is just a statistical illusion—it doesn’t mean the content is factual.

This is why it's risky to let AI act as an *expert*, rather than a *supportive assistant*. A good assistant can say, “I’ll check on that” or “I’m not sure.” A good AI should too—but most aren’t built that way (yet).

**Assistant:**

AI like ChatGPT doesn’t actually know anything—it just predicts what words should come next based on patterns in data. That means it also doesn’t know when it *doesn’t* know something. Instead of pausing or admitting uncertainty, it confidently fills in the blanks, even if it’s completely wrong. This is what people call "hallucination," but really, it’s just the machine doing exactly what it was trained to do: talk, not think.

The problem is, we often mistake fluency for truth. When an AI speaks in clear, authoritative language, it sounds trustworthy—even when it's not. Unlike humans, it won’t hesitate, show doubt, or say “I’m not sure” unless explicitly instructed. That inability to stay silent is exactly what makes it risky. In critical situations, we don’t just need answers—we need systems that *know when to shut up*.
